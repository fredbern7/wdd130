Detailed Trip Descriptions

Trip 1: Beginner Whitewater Rafting

This introductory trip is ideal for newcomers, families, or anyone looking for a gentle introduction to rafting. Over approximately 2 hours, you'll float down a scenic section of the Colorado River, encountering Class I-II rapids that provide just enough excitement without overwhelming beginners. Your journey starts with a safety briefing from certified guides who will teach paddling techniques and river reading skills. Along the way, enjoy breathtaking views of towering canyons, wildlife sightings like eagles and deer, and crystal-clear waters. All necessary equipment, including life jackets, helmets, and paddles, is provided. This trip emphasizes fun, education, and relaxation, making it perfect for building confidence on the water. Departure times are available in the morning and afternoon, with a minimum age of 6 years old.

Trip 2: Multi-Day Rafting Expedition

For those seeking an immersive adventure, this 5-day expedition takes you deep into the iconic Grand Canyon via the Colorado River. You'll tackle a mix of Class III-IV rapids, including famous ones like Lava Falls, while camping each night on sandy beaches under star-filled skies. Days are filled with rafting thrills, optional hikes to ancient ruins and waterfalls, and educational talks on geology and Native American history from knowledgeable guides. Meals are prepared fresh, with options for dietary needs, and all camping gear is supplied. This trip requires moderate physical fitness and is best for intermediate rafters aged 12 and up. Trip 3: Advanced Whitewater Rafting

Designed for experienced thrill-seekers, this full-day advanced trip on Oregon's Rogue River delivers heart-pounding action through Class IV-V rapids like Blossom Bar and Rainie Falls. Over 8-10 hours, you'll maneuver technical sections with powerful waves, narrow chutes, and steep drops, all under the guidance of expert rafting professionals who prioritize safety with ongoing communication and rescue training. The river winds through pristine wilderness, offering glimpses of black bears, otters, and old-growth forests. Participants should have prior rafting experience, strong swimming skills, and be at least 16 years old. Gear includes high-performance rafts, wetsuits for cooler waters, and helmets. This trip is about pushing limits while connecting with untamed nature.

Trip 4: Family Scenic Float on the Snake River

This easy-going trip is perfect for families and those seeking a relaxed rafting experience. Over 3 hours, you'll gently float down the Snake River in Wyoming, enjoying stunning views of the Teton Mountains, wildlife spotting opportunities including moose and bald eagles, and calm waters with minimal rapids (Class I). Guided by friendly experts, this trip includes fun facts about the local ecosystem and history. All equipment is provided, and it's suitable for all ages, including young children.
